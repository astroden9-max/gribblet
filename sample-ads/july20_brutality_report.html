THE JULY 20, 2026 CRACKDOWN: SYSTEMIC POLICE BRUTALITY & EXCESSES

On July 20, 2026, security forces deployed an unprecedented level of force against students and youth participating in the Cockroach Janta Party’s (CJP) peaceful 'Sansad Chalo' march. The following report documents verified eyewitness testimony, hospital casualty records, and legal affidavits regarding excessive force, illegal tactics, and civil rights violations by Delhi Police, Railway Protection Force (RPF), and paramilitary personnel.

■ Direct Physical Violence & Assaults

[Targeted Aggression] ──> [Impact Weapons] ──> [Severe Traumas (RML / Lady Hardinge)]
  • Indiscriminate Lathi-Charge: Security personnel unleashed widespread, unprovoked baton charges across Jantar Mantar, Patel Chowk, Janpath, and Connaught Place. Marchers were struck on the head, neck, and upper torso rather than below the waist, resulting in severe head trauma, cracked ribs, broken limbs, and internal bleeding.
  • Over 100 Hospitalized: Over 38 injured demonstrators were admitted to Lady Hardinge Medical College, while more than 65 were rushed to Dr. Ram Manohar Lohia (RML) Hospital. Emergency room logs cited skull fractures, lacerations, and severe ocular inflammation caused by tear gas fired directly into crowds.
  • Targeting Women Protesters: Multiple female students were violently assaulted by male officers without female personnel present. Key figures, including Gitanjali Angmo (wife of fasting activist Sonam Wangchuk), were dragged by their hair, pushed, and physically knocked down during the police sweep.
  • Assault on Public Figures & Support Vehicles: Police targeted a central support vehicle and truck carrying CJP founder Abhijeet Dipke, Shabana Azmi, Prakash Raj, and Gitanjali Angmo. Officers beat non-violent occupants, forcefully dragged demonstrators off the vehicle, and damaged equipment.

■ Concealment of Identity & Illegal Tactics

  • Violation of Mandated Identification Protocols & Plainclothes Deployment: Personnel from the Delhi Police, RPF, Nagaland Armed Police, and paramilitary units operated inside the protest area in plainclothes / civil dress and without name tags or service badges. When confronted by student marchers about missing nameplates, officers claimed they had "dropped off during the scuffle." Ground monitors and independent legal observers recorded plainclothes officers and unbadged personnel wielding wooden batons, beating non-violent student marchers including women, and stripping off identification tags to prevent legal accountability.
  • Use of Modified Weapons: Physical evidence gathered by medical teams and eyewitness accounts revealed police utilizing batons fitted with exposed nails and metal ends, causing deep puncture wounds and severe lacerations to non-violent student marchers.
  • Communication & Blackout Tactic: Authorities deployed mobile internet jammers across Central Delhi. By severing mobile data, police intentionally restricted live broadcasting, emergency coordination, and immediate reporting of police conduct.

■ Over-Confinement & Dangerous Crowd Control

Tactic Used Operational Implication Public Risk & Injury Outcome
Welded Barricades Metal perimeter walls were welded together to trap marchers within narrow corridors. Created severe bottleneck choke points, triggering near-stampede conditions for tens of thousands of trapped students.
Tear Gas Enclosure Tear gas canisters were lobbed directly into densely packed, enclosed crowds. Caused widespread asphyxiation, panic, and ocular injuries as trapped protesters had no exit routes.
Transit Blockade Shutting five major Delhi Metro stations (Rajiv Chowk, Janpath, Patel Chowk, Central Secretariat, Seva Tirth). Blocked dispersal routes, forcing peaceful marchers back toward aggressive police lines.

■ Stage Destruction & Post-Protest Harassment

  • Dismantling of Peaceful Site: In the late evening, police forces swept through Jantar Mantar, forcibly dismantling the central stage where Sonam Wangchuk’s hunger strike had been hosted. Personal belongings, mattresses, carpets, and medical supplies intended for fasting leaders were confiscated or destroyed.
  • Deniability & Misleading Statements: Despite overwhelming physical evidence and medical logs, official statements issued by Delhi Police denied targeted assaults, rejected reports of widespread injuries, and claimed their actions were executed strictly with "professional restraint"—a claim directly contested in Public Interest Litigations (PILs) filed with the Delhi High Court on July 22.
08:00 AM METRO & MOBILIZATION

Transit Blockades & Five Station Shutdowns

To stop arriving students, police order the shutdown of five major Delhi Metro stations: Rajiv Chowk, Janpath, Patel Chowk, Central Secretariat, and Seva Tirth. Trains pass without opening doors, forcing marchers to navigate security checkpoints on foot.

11:30 AM COMMUNICATION BLACKOUT

Signal Jammers Deployed Across Central Delhi

As over 100,000 protesters gather near Jantar Mantar and Raisina Road, mobile internet and cellular service are cut using signal jammers. Live broadcasts halt and communication between organizer groups is severed.

02:00 PM SANSAD MARCH CRACKDOWN

Welded Barricades, Tear Gas & Heavy Lathi-Charge

When marchers advance toward Parliament, security forces deploy multi-tiered welded steel barricades. Unbadged personnel and plainclothes officers execute baton charges and release tear gas directly into dense crowds, causing stampede conditions and widespread injuries.

04:30 PM CASUALTIES & DELEGATION

Over 100 Hospitalized; Delegation Meets Minister J.P. Nadda

Over 100 injured students are transferred to RML and Lady Hardinge hospitals with head injuries and broken limbs. Concurrently, CJP spokespersons meet Union Health Minister J.P. Nadda to deliver student demands, including Dharmendra Pradhan's resignation.

08:30 PM STAGE SWEEP

Police Dismantle Jantar Mantar Stage & Confiscate Supplies

Police sweep the Jantar Mantar protest site, forcibly tearing down temporary tents, stages, and sound equipment, while confiscating medical kits and bedding. Protesters regroup nearby to maintain an overnight vigil.

⚠️ DOCUMENTATION NOTICE: All physical reports, eyewitness affidavits, and hospital casualty logs from July 20 have been submitted to the Delhi High Court under preservation orders.
